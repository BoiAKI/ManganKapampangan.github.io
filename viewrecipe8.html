TIBUK-TIBUK

ORIGIN

Besides their main dishes, Kapampangans also take their desserts seriously. One dessert that you should try when visiting Pampanga is tibuk-tibuk. This is Pampanga’s version of Maja Blanca that is traditionally made with carabao’s milk, with a hint of dayap zest (local lemon) with latik on top. Tibuk-tibuk or tibok is literally translated as “heart beat” that describes the bubbles pulsating up and down (that looks like a beating heart) when the pudding is cooked. We suggest you try Susie’s Cuisine’s version of the tibuk-tibuk, for that creamy, melt in your mouth goodness that tastes like heaven.

RECIPE

  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 4 cups fresh carabao's milk (or cow's milk)
  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt (if using cow's milk)

PROCEDURE

  • Prepare Coconut Cream: In a pan over medium heat, bring coconut cream to a boil. Cook until it thickens, stirring occasionally.
  • Simmer Coconut Cream: Lower the heat and simmer until oil separates and solids form, stirring regularly to prevent burning. Cook until curds turn golden brown.
  • Drain Latik: Use a fine mesh sieve or colander to drain the latik, reserving the oil.
  • Prepare Pan: Brush a 7 x 5-inch pan generously with coconut oil and set aside.
  • Prepare Milk Mixture: In a heavy-bottomed pot, combine milk, rice flour, sugar, and salt (if using cow's milk). Whisk until smooth.
  • Cook Milk Mixture: Over medium-low heat, bring the mixture to a simmer, whisking regularly. Cook for 10 to 15 minutes until it thickens to a smooth paste.
  • Transfer to Pan: Gently transfer the milk mixture to the prepared pan. Smooth it out with a spatula to ensure even distribution.
  • Cool and Set: Allow the mixture to cool and set in the pan.
  • Finish with Latik: Once set, brush the top with coconut oil and garnish with latik.
  • Serve: Cut into serving slices and enjoy your Bibingkang Malagkit with Latik!

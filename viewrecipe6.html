KARE-KARE

ORIGIN

The history of Kare-Kare Kapampangan is deeply rooted in the culinary traditions of Pampanga, which is known as the "Culinary Capital of the Philippines." Traditionally, Kare-kare is made up of beef, oxtail, peanut sauce, and vegetables like eggplant, pechay, heart of banana and green beans. The perfect partner of kare-kare is the shrimp paste (also known as bagoong). In Pampanga, Kare-Kare has its own distinct version known as Kare-Kare Kapampangan. What sets it apart is the use of achuete (annatto) seeds, which give the dish its signature orange color, and the inclusion of more native vegetables like puso ng saging (banana blossom) and alugbati (Malabar spinach). Kare-Kare Kapampangan has become a beloved dish not only in Pampanga but also throughout the Philippines, often served during special occasions and celebrations as a symbol of Filipino culinary heritage and tradition .

RECIPE

  • 1 pound oxtail
  • ½ pound beef tripe
  • 1 eggplant
  • 3 pieces bok choy (cut in the middle)
  • Sitaw beans (can substitute with green beans)
  • 1 onion
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 tablespoons peanut butter (creamy)
  • Pepper and salt to taste
  • Bay leaves
  • Mamasita’s Kare-Kare Mix
  • Can substitute with Ground Annatto powder

PROCEDURE

  • Prepare Meat: Wash and cut oxtail and tripe, boil until tender, skimming off scum.
  • Make Peanut Sauce: Toast ground rice, sauté garlic and onions, add ground rice, peanut butter, and water until thickened.
  • Combine Meat and Sauce: Add peanut sauce to tender meat, simmer to meld flavors.
  • Add Vegetables: Blanch eggplant, string beans, and banana blossoms until slightly tender, add to pot and simmer until cooked.
  • Adjust Seasoning: Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, sugar, or bagoong.
  • Serve: Serve hot with bagoong alamang and rice.

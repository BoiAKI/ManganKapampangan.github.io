CAMARU

ORIGIN

Filipino exotic dishes often feature unexpected ingredients, such as the Adobong Kamaru, a popular culinary tradition in Pampanga. This dish uses a mole cricket, typically 3-5 cm long, as the main ingredient, and sometimes served with tomatoes. Although scientifically proven edible, it is recommended to remove the harder outer wings and legs before consumption. Mole crickets are harmless indoors and can eat vegetables, making them a favorite pulutan for Kapampangans. Seasonal in the Philippines, mole crickets are often available in popular Filipino restaurants. A 2008 study found that five ounces of mole crickets provide 28% of the daily protein and 74% of the calories.

RECIPE

  • ¼ kilo of kamaru (mole crickets)
  • 3 pcs tomatoess
  • 1 medium size onion sliced
  • ¼ cup minced garlic
  • ¼ cup vinegar
  • 2-5 pcs chili ( labuyo )
  • 1 tsp salt and pepper

PROCEDURE

  • Wash the Kamaru thoroughly to remove dirt and impurities, or soak them in water/vinegar if needed.
  • Prepare garlic, onion, tomatoes, siling labuyo, vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  • Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  • Add tomatoes and siling labuyo, cook until tomatoes soften.
  • Incorporate cleaned Kamaru, mix well.
  • Simmer for 5 minutes to meld flavors.
  • Add vinegar, let it boil for 2 minutes without stirring.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Serve hot with rice or as a side dish.

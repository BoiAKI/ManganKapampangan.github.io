BATUTE TUGAK

ORIGIN

They also call this as "tugak" around Pampanga. It tastes like chicken and it's really good. Farmers in Pampanga used to depend on rain water to irrigater their farms. Children would then catch the frogs, which came out during the rainy season, while their parents cultivated the land or planted rice. Outwitting the frogs has been a traditional "family bonding" ritual. Betute is a play of words on butete, which means "tadpole" in the local dialect. Betute is the entire frog stuffed with minced pork - so it looks like a very fat frog. It is then deep-fried in oil.

RECIPE

  • 3-5 pcs. native frog, cleaned & skinned
  • Salt & pepper
  • 1/2 k. ground pork
  • 2 tsps. garlic, minced
  • 1 pc. onion, chopped
  • 2 pcs. tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Onion leeks, chopped (optional)
    • >

PROCEDURE

  • Season frog with salt and pepper and set aside.
  • Saut√© the ground pork with garlic, onion, and tomato.
  • When cooked, add the vinegar.
  • Let simmer and also let the raw taste of vinegar evaporate.
  • After 3 minutes, season with salt and pepper.
  • When done, put on a platter.
  • Stuff frog with the above cooked ingredients then powder frog with flour.
  • Deep fry.
